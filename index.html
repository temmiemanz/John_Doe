ACTIVE WORD
_
ABILITY — PERSPECTIVE

John Doe speaks one word. It requires one living perceiver and one target — living or not. The perceiver's reality shifts to match the word as they personally interpret it, but only as it applies to the target. The sun exists, the rock floats — the perceiver alone experiences this. Everyone else sees what is actually happening.

LIMITS
  • Two required — one living perceiver + one target (living or not). The word is placed on the target, seen only by the perceiver.
  • One perceiver only — if both are living, only one can be the perceiver. You cannot make two people affect each other simultaneously.
  • One at a time — one word, one pair, one effect. A new word replaces the last entirely.
  • Scope — the word only affects something the perceiver can directly perceive or interact with in that moment. The sun, the earth, the sky are outside scope entirely.
  • Interpretation — what the word means to the perceiver is what happens, not what John Doe intends.
  • Roll dependent — outcome is as precise or as chaotic as the roll allows.

Lore ▼

John Doe isn't a name. It's a placeholder. He woke up with no memories, no identity, and no idea who he was before whatever happened to him happened. His given name is unknown, even to him. The people around him had to call him something.

He's not broken in any obvious way. His mind works fine. He can think, learn, hold a conversation. What's missing is everything underneath that. No personality shaped by years of experience. No sense of self. No history to draw meaning from. He can make new memories, but they sit on top of nothing.

The apocalypse didn't wait for him to figure it out. Now he's trying to survive a collapsed world while quietly coming apart at the seams, not from the violence, but from the question he can't stop asking: who am I?
